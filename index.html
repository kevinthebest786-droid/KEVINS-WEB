The Importance of Fasting (Sawm)

Fasting is one of the Five Pillars of Islam that builds discipline, strengthens faith, and develops character.

What is Fasting?

Fasting, known as Sawm, requires Muslims to abstain from food, drink, and sinful behavior from dawn until sunset during Ramadan. It develops Taqwa (God-consciousness) and strengthens the spiritual connection with Allah.

Benefits of Fasting

Spiritual Growth

Increases Taqwa and strengthens faith through worship and reflection.

Self-Discipline

Teaches control over desires, speech, and behavior.

Gratitude

Helps appreciate blessings like food, health, and family.

Quranic Reference

"O you who believe! Fasting has been prescribed upon you as it was prescribed upon those before you, that you may become righteous."

— Surah Al-Baqarah 2:183

Ramadan

  • • Suhoor – Pre-dawn meal
  • • Iftar – Breaking the fast at sunset
  • • Taraweeh – Special night prayers
  • • Laylatul Qadr – Night of Power

Ramadan is a month of reflection, charity, and devotion. Muslims increase prayer, read the Quran, and strengthen community bonds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who must fast?

All adult Muslims who are physically and mentally able.

Who is exempt?

The sick, travelers, elderly, pregnant women, and children.

What is the goal of fasting?

To achieve Taqwa and become spiritually stronger.
